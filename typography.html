Typography - Processes Book













For the theme of my book, I was inspired by the Kufic script, which is an Arabic script dated back to the 7th to 10th centuries. Kufic script is characterized by angular, rectilinear letterforms and its horizontal orientation. There are many different versions of Kufic script. I used an English typeface inspired by the Kufic for the book cover design as well as throughout the book as graphic elements. For the color palette I kept it simple and neat by using only two colors, dark blue and light beige color. Those two colors contrast well and give clarity and balance to the design.






Creating Letterforms Postcard



I picked (Source Serif) typeface as the origin of my prototype. In recreating the typeface I got my inspiration from Arabic Kufic Script. I tried to keep the boldness of the stork and stem relatively the same to the Source Serif font. However, I edited the serif, terminal and finial of the letters to give it sharper and more geometric look. I laid out the letters to spell (harvest moon). I picked three hues; green, light green and pink.













Type Anatomy








I tried to capture the phrase meaning through out the whole design. For the outer panels, I went with dark blue and little white dots representing a night sky. The cover layout have a lot of negative space to convey the vastness of the sky. The center back cover that included short paragraph about Times font development story. I also placed different letters in random manner as if they are stars. The warm gradient of orange color in the inside panels is chosen to represent sunrise. A simple sun illustration works as a focal point in the center panel when the brochure is opened.







Type Classification Poster





I begin my process by researching various fonts from different type classifications. I picked three fonts with distinctive characteristic to be highlighted in my posters. I studied those typefaces closely and look for design inspirations from the shapes of the letters and their anatomies. Through out my design, I tried to emphasize and showcase the some of the features of some letters from each of the three typefaces. For the color palette, I mainly used black and white to give the contrast that can help demonstrate the letters clearly. The golden yellow color was used to break the color tone, and to highlight some of the elements in the posters.
